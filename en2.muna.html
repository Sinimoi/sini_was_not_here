MUNA

2011

Muna ("The Egg") was an apparatus made in a course about Experimental Anatomy.
With action, sound and movement I tried to represent
how it might feel like, when an egg travels through a body.
I wanted to construct such anatomy, where
the focus is on movement, sound and interaction
instead of a clear and stable form.
The movements, the sounds and the pains reflect from one spot to another.


When you hit forcefully with a rubber-headed club to
a bottom of a barrel,
A bone attached to another end of a shaft attached to the barrel
hit a tin can, out of which the egg started rolling, clattering and rattling,
at times smoothly sliding along the gutter,
eventually jumping out into a bowl.

It felt empowering to hit hard onto the barrel,
which boomed, swang and got the egg loose.
